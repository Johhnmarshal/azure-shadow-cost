## What to do next

1. **Open the Reservations blade.** Confirm per-SKU savings — the 30% blended
   estimate is conservative; large families often break above that.
2. **Cross-check against Peak Rightsizing.** Never reserve a family×region
   that should be downsized first — locks in current inefficiency for 1-3 years.
3. **Buy serially, not in bulk.** Two reservations a quarter beats six at
   once: it spreads renewal risk and lets you re-baseline before the next pick.
4. **Raise the buffer when ready.** The binding constraint here is the 5,000
   procurement cap. At 10,000 buffer, the shortlist would still be the same
   four picks (none over-buffer in this dataset) — but at 1,000 buffer, only
   the Bs and Fsv2 picks would fit.

## Decision rules used

```
STABLE_CV_MAX:    0.15   # CV under 15% → VM RI 1Y, commit 80% of PAYG
VARIABLE_CV_MAX:  0.30   # CV 15-30%   → Compute SP 1Y, commit 65%
                         # CV ≥ 30%    → Compute SP 1Y deferred, commit 30% (HIGH risk)
RI_1Y_SAVINGS:    0.30   # blended; confirm per-SKU at commit time
SP_1Y_SAVINGS:    0.17
CANCEL_FEE_PCT:   0.12   # Microsoft's current cancellation fee
```

These are the open-source FinOps Engine defaults. Override per-deployment via
the SPA's RI Coverage tab (buffer only) or the constants in `ri_coverage.py`.
